    Banff

    Banff is a resort town and one of Canada's most popular tourist destinations. Known for its mountainous surroundings and hot springs, it is a destination for outdoor sports and features extensive hiking, biking, scrambling and skiing destinations within the area.

    Customer Rating: 4.85/5

    Emerald Lake

    Peaceful lake nestled in the mountains and surrounded by lush, green forest. It is the largest of Yoho's 61 lakes and ponds, as well as one of the park's premier tourist attractions. Emerald Lake Lodge, a high-end lodge perched on the edge of the lake, provides local accommodation.

    Customer Rating: 4.5/5

    Moraine Lake

    Moraine Lake is a glacially fed lake in Banff National Park, 14 kilometres outside the village of Lake Louise, Alberta, Canada. It is situated in the Valley of the Ten Peaks, at an elevation of approximately 1,884 metres.

    Customer Rating: 4.8/5

    Mount Albert

    Mount Albert is an exurban community located within the Town of East Gwillimbury. The downtown area is centred at Centre Street and Main Street. The Mount Albert Creek passes through Mount Albert.

    Customer Rating: 4.65/5
